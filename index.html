How to Read This Report
🎯 What is this dashboard?

This is the Odoo vs Website Update View Reconciliation Dashboard — a daily inventory health check that compares what Cityfurnish has recorded in Odoo (ERP) against what is actually live and visible on the website for customers to rent.

The core question it answers: "Is the inventory quantity visible to customers on the website matching what we have recorded in our system?"

Goal: The Gap between Odoo and Website should ideally be 0. Any mismatch means customers are either seeing incorrect availability or items that should be shown aren't being displayed.

🏷️ What do the Status badges mean?
StatusMeaningImpact
Publish Product is live and visible on the Cityfurnish website. Customers can see it and rent it. Active rentable inventory. Gaps here directly affect customer experience.
UnPublish Product exists in Odoo but is temporarily hidden — under maintenance, refurbishment, or pending restock. Low urgency. Not visible to customers but tracked in Odoo.
Not Available Product exists in Odoo and has qty in the Website Update View (mediator), but is never actually listed on the website. Customers cannot see or rent it — no product page exists for it. Qty flows through the system but is invisible to customers. Needs to be listed or removed from the update view.
🔍 Why is Odoo data filtered?

Not all Odoo records represent rentable inventory. The filter ensures only clean, active stock is counted:

FilterWhy
Condition: New + OkOnly items in good rentable condition. Damaged or returned items are excluded.
Movement: InOnly items currently in the warehouse. Outbound/rented-out items are not counted.
Exclude JDHJodhpur is a non-operational warehouse — not part of the active city network.
Exclude AssetsInternal operational assets (tools, packaging) are not rentable products.
⚡ Understanding the Gap — Two Different Metrics
Net Gap (KPI card) — Total Odoo qty minus Total Website qty across all products. Positive and negative gaps cancel out. e.g. net −14 means website shows 14 more units in total than Odoo.

Mismatches (table summary) — Count of individual SKUs where Odoo ≠ Website. e.g. 45 SKUs have some gap, but some are +10 and some are −11 — they largely cancel, leaving a small net gap.

Example: SKU A gap = +10, SKU B gap = −11. Net gap = −1. Mismatches = 2. Both correct — different views of the same data.

The DoD view tracks how Odoo and Website quantities change every day over the last 10 working days. It helps spot trends — are quantities consistently dropping? Is the gap widening?

❓ Frequently Asked Questions
Why does the same SKU have different quantities in Odoo vs Website? ▼
The website shows a threshold-based quantity (often a fixed number per city regardless of actual stock), while Odoo shows the true physical count. The website quantity is an availability signal, not an exact real-time count. This is why small gaps are expected and normal.
Why are some items only in Odoo and not on the website? ▼
New products added to Odoo may not yet be configured on the website. Discontinued items may be in Odoo for record-keeping but unpublished. Items in damaged/returned condition are in Odoo but never shown on the website.
What does a very large positive gap mean? ▼
A large positive gap (Odoo much higher than website) usually means inventory exists in the warehouse but hasn't been published on the website yet. This is a lost rental opportunity — the product catalogue team needs to update the website listing.
What does a large negative gap mean? ▼
A large negative gap (website higher than Odoo) may indicate the website is showing stale/old quantities that haven't been refreshed, or physical stock was moved/damaged without updating Odoo. This can lead to failed order fulfilment and customer complaints.
What is JDH and why is it excluded? ▼
JDH refers to the Jodhpur warehouse, which is not part of Cityfurnish's active rental city network. It is excluded to avoid inflating inventory numbers with stock not available to customers.
Why do some products start with a # symbol? ▼
The # prefix is a naming convention in the Odoo product catalogue to indicate internal product groupings or categories. It has no operational significance.
How often should this report be run? ▼
This report is designed for daily use. The DoD tab tracks the last 10 days, so running it every day gives a rolling trend view. Recommended time: end-of-day or first thing in the morning before operations start.
